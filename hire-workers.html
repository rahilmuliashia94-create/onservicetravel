Our 4-Step International Hiring Process

1. Define Your Hiring Needs

Tell us about your role requirements including job responsibilities, contract length, start date, location, language requirements, and any certifications required.

2. Candidate Sourcing and Screening

We identify qualified candidates through our recruitment network and conduct:

  • Skills assessments and practical tests
  • Government ID verification
  • Background checks where permitted
  • Reference validation
  • Video interviews and soft-skill evaluation

Only verified candidates proceed to employer interviews.

3. Compliance, Permits and Travel Coordination

Once a candidate is selected, we coordinate visa applications, employer documentation, permit processing, and travel logistics in partnership with local immigration specialists. Workers also complete medical checks and pre-departure orientation where required.

4. Arrival and Onboarding

Upon arrival, workers complete orientation and integration support. Employers receive candidate documentation packages and onboarding guidance, while our team monitors the first 30–90 days to ensure a smooth transition.

Compliance and Risk Management

International hiring involves legal responsibility. Our compliance framework includes:

  • Partnerships with licensed immigration and legal specialists in destination countries
  • Verification of worker eligibility and documentation in accordance with local laws
  • Support for employer declarations, insurance requirements, and medical checks where required
  • Clear written agreements outlining worker responsibilities, employer obligations, and guarantee terms

Each hire is supported by a complete audit trail including application records, verification reports, permit submissions, and arrival confirmations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you ensure legal compliance?
We coordinate all recruitment and documentation with verified immigration partners and local legal professionals to ensure every placement complies with national labor and immigration regulations.

Yes. Employers can conduct live interviews, review recorded introductions, or request skill demonstrations before making a hiring decision.

Do you provide temporary or permanent workers?
Both options are available. We recruit seasonal workers, fixed-term contract employees, and long-term hires depending on employer needs and immigration regulations.

What language skills do candidates have?
Each candidate profile includes assessed language proficiency. Additional pre-departure language coaching can be arranged where roles require customer interaction.

What happens if a worker is not a good fit?
If the placement does not work within the guarantee period, we provide a replacement candidate or service credit according to the contract terms.
